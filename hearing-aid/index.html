Hearing Aid

Sundays are harder days to write.

I don't know why.

Maybe it's because I should take the day off.

A day of rest.

But this is not really laborious.

This is play time.

And so I sits.

So I writes.

Oh, had a miracle happen the other day.

Whole family in our vehicle.

Driving around after some soaking of sun.

Wanted to hit the local grocery store so we turned down a road we typically do not use.

It's that season so lots of construction.

We are waiting at a red at a T. 

Can only go left or right.

We're in the left turn lane.

Light turns green, I look left and right, all clear, and I start to go.

Now the lane I'm turning onto has two lanes. I usually turn left into the lane on the right, since I'll have to make a right soon after into the store.

Make sense?

So I'm turning and I hear my wife say, calmly but authoritatively, "Turn left. Turn left."

So I turn it tight to the left, getting into the left side of the left lane, and we see a pickup truck fly by us just missing our front bumper. 

Lady ran a red.

She never stopped.

Didn't seem to even slow down.

My wife says she gave us a nasty look.

Typical, right?

She had a big dog sticking its head out by her side, so we hypothesize maybe she was distracted. 

But who knows.

Here's the weird part.

Both my son and my wife agree that my wife never said a word.

She thinks she gasped and raised her hand up to the right side of her window in an attempt to shield herself.

But didn't say a word.

And my son agrees saying she said nothing.

But what I remember vividly and distinctly is she said "Turn left. Turn left."

Twice.

And not panicked.

Calm.

Direct.

Just to do it.

So I did.

Hard.

And the flying dog lady truck missed us.

We had a terrific boost of adrenaline and spent the rest of our day just praising.

Thankful.

Near misses are too often and quickly forgotten.

Or at least, not fully appreciated.

I don't want to do that.

So I'll write it down here. 

To remind me to cherish it.

What's weird is this isn't the first time for me.

I had another similar miracle happen to me decades ago.

When I was a teenager.

Also on the road.

A voice told me to stop.

So I did.

But that time, I was in the car all by myself.
